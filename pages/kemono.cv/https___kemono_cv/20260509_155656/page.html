What Does Kemono Mean in Japanese?

Short answer

Kemono literally translates to “beast” or “animal” in Japanese, often referring to creatures in folklore and modern pop culture that blend human and animal traits, celebrating anthropomorphism across art, anime, and digital platforms.

Etymology and Cultural Significance

The word kemono (獣) has ancient roots in Japanese folklore, where shapeshifting foxes (kitsune) or tanuki would blur the lines between human and animal. Today, the term embraces everything from mythic beasts in classical literature to the modern fandom of animal‑personified characters.

Kemono embodies Japan’s enduring fascination with nature’s wild side, inviting us to explore identity beyond the human form. — Dr. Yuki Tanaka, Professor of Japanese Folklore
